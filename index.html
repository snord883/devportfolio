About Me

I’ve always been fascinated by the potential of AI, and even more so with Reinforcement Learning (RL). There’s something incredibly rewarding about teaching a system to learn, much like teaching a child a new task or game. My journey started with teaching my computer to play mancala and presenting on how RL can solve real-world problems. This ultimately motivated me to pursue a master’s at Georgia Tech. The challenge of structuring rewards to guide a system to learn and adapt captivated me, and RL has since become my favorite area of AI. I’m drawn to it because it feels like an uncharted frontier in machine learning, with a lot of potential for innovation and research.


In addition to my RL focus, I also took a course called “AI for Robotics” that I loved. I’m intrigued by how RL can be applied to robotics and I’m interested to explore how it can contribute to a variety of AI applications.


I’m always looking for opportunities to learn and grow. I’d love to connect with mentors or professionals to brainstorm ideas and collaborate. I’m open to relocation for the right opportunity and am considering pursuing a PhD in RL in the future. Until then, I’ll continue building projects and staying curious.
